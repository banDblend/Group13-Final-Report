Korea

In South Korea's fall, trees paint the landscape with vibrant hues, inviting outdoor activities like hiking and picnics in cool weather. Harvest festivals add a festive touch, showcasing traditional dishes with autumn flavors. The season offers a serene atmosphere, perfect for leisurely strolls to soak in the beauty of nature's.

Fall Foliage Viewing: Many Koreans enjoy taking trips to mountains, parks, and other scenic areas to appreciate the changing leaves. Popular locations for this activity include Namsan Park in Seoul.

Harvest Festivals: Various festivals take place to celebrate the abundance of crops. These festivals often include traditional performances, food tastings, and agricultural exhibitions.

Chuseok: It's a time for families to come together, pay respects to ancestors, and share traditional Korean food. Many people travel to their hometowns to spend time with their families during this holiday.

Camping: People often head to campsites in the mountains or near rivers to enjoy the crisp air and the beauty of the autumn landscape.

Japan

In Japan's fall, trees paint the landscape with bright colors. The weather is cool and pleasant, perfect for exploring places like Kyoto and Nikko with their beautiful temples surrounded by fall foliage. People celebrate the season with traditional festivals and enjoy the beauty of autumn leaves during "momiji-gari." And, of course, fall treats include delicious seasonal foods like chestnuts and sweet potatoes, adding to the cultural charm of this enchanting time of year.

Autumn Leaf Viewing (Momijigari): Immerse yourself in the breathtaking beauty of Japan's autumn by embarking on a journey to witness the vibrant foliage. Explore iconic parks, serene gardens, and picturesque mountains that transform into a kaleidoscope of colors during the enchanting season of "koyo."

Delight your taste buds with the flavors of autumn in Japan. Indulge in dishes crafted from seasonal ingredients such as sweet potatoes, chestnuts, and mushrooms. Experience the warmth of Japanese hospitality through traditional hot pots and grilled specialties as the weather turns cooler.

Indulge in the ultimate relaxation by visiting Onsen Retreats. The contrast of the crisp air and warm, soothing waters creates a truly rejuvenating experience. Consider staying in a ryokan, a traditional Japanese inn, for an authentic onsen experience.

Experience the beauty of Japan's landscapes through Scenic Train Rides. The fall foliage adds an extra layer of charm to journeys such as the Arashiyama Scenic Railway in Kyoto or the Nakasendo Trail in the Kiso Valley.

China

In China's fall, leaves turn golden, and the weather is nice for outdoor activities. Famous places like the Great Wall and the Yellow Mountains look stunning with fall colors. Families celebrate the Mid-Autumn Festival, enjoying mooncakes and the full moon. The season also brings a harvest of tasty fruits and vegetables.

Visit the Great Wall of China during autumn to enjoy cooler temperatures and stunning landscapes. The fall foliage adds a touch of vibrant colors to the surroundings, making your hike or cable car ride even more picturesque.

Li River in Guilin offers picturesque karst landscapes, surrounded by autumnal hues, create a mesmerizing experience. The tranquil waters and unique limestone formations are particularly enchanting during this season.

Experience the vibrant Mid-Autumn Festival, a significant traditional Chinese celebration. Join locals in moon-viewing activities, indulge in mooncakes, and witness lantern displays. Festivities often include cultural performances and parades.

Visit ancient temples and historical sites, such as the Forbidden City in Beijing or the Terracotta Army in Xi'an. The cooler weather in autumn makes exploring these cultural treasures more comfortable.
