SUMMER

Japan

Across much of Japan, the summer is characterized by hot and humid weather. Taking refuge from the heat, many locals opt for cooler climates near the ocean or up in the mountains. As the heat begins to wane in the evenings, people partake in regional fireworks displays, traditional song and dance, and centuries-old festivals.

Every summer, hikers from across the world journey to Japan to embark on a trek up its most iconic peak, Mt. Fuji.

Ranked within the top three best fireworks displays in Japan, the Nagaoka Matsuri in Niigata Prefecture is a two-day celebration featuring some 20,000 fireworks in a typical year.

From rugged river valleys nestled in the mountains to white-capped waves washing onto tropical beaches, Japan is full of thrilling water sports and recreation.

Traditional summer festivals provide an opportunity to witness local culture firsthand. Towns across the country present one-of-a-kind celebrations featuring traditional mikoshi floats, dancing, costumes, and ample amounts of food and drink.

Korea

The first half of summer in Korea is hot and humid, with high chances of rain. Rainy season runs from mid June to late July. However, it’s not always raining, but there is a high chance of it. Evenings are cooler, but still warm.

Without a doubt, this has to be the best of all summer activities in Korea. Grab your swimwear and suncream, find a spot on the sandy shore, and sleep. Try out some beach sports and other activities to work up a sweat.

Summer is one of the best times to get out and join a festival. The Boryeong Mud Festival has mud wrestling, mud bathing, extreme mud sports, K-pop concerts, fireworks, a water park, and lots of great beachside restaurants.

Once the sun goes down, the weather cools a lot and it’s the perfect time to explore more. You can join the fortress walls in many places throughout Seoul and two of the easiest ones are from Hyehwa Station (Naksan Park) or Dongdameun Station (Heunginjimin Gate).

The Han River is the perfect place for summer activities in Korea, both day and night. During the day it’s a great place to see local Seoulites enjoying a picnic on the grass, riding, skating, swimming, and generally relaxing.

Thailand

Thailand has a great number of destinations to visit in summer that you should never miss. From the amazing islands, magnificent coastlines to the impressive events and festivals. Thailand in summer has everything you need for entertaining, relaxing and experiencing.

Kwai River Bridge is a famous cultural destination in Thailand. Built by prisoners of war, this bridge is now a memorial for died forced labourers.

Khao Yai is the third-largest national park in Thailand which is most suitable for independent visitors. The mix of jungles, waterfalls and rivers has made it a perfect destination for visitors to explore nature.

Sukhothai is known as the first capital of Siam. As one of the most attractive archaeological destinations in Thailand, Sukhothai attracts many tourists annually for its splendid old town.

Phi Ta Khon which is known as Ghost Festival, this festival is held in Dan Sai Town on the weekend of the lunar calendar’s 6th full moon. At Phi Ta Khon Festival, you can experience the vibe of a religious festival in Thailand.

Malaysia

The hot and humid weather is especially apparent around June to August, making it an excellent time to pack up your travel gears and start your wonderful journey of discovery.

Sipadan Island is surrounded by white sandy beaches and some of the most diverse reefs and marine life. Sipadan Island ranked as one of the top diving destinations of the world

Taman Negara which is the 130-million-year-old virgin rainforest is one of world’s oldest and has grown into a significant eco-tourism attraction in Malaysia.

Escape the summer heat at one of the highlands located outside of Kuala Lumpur. Genting Highlands is home to hotels, casinos, shopping malls, as well as an indoor and outdoor theme park.

Mount Kinabalu is the highest mountain in Malaysia, located in Sabah. The mountain stands at a humongous height of 4,101 m and is the most accessible mountain in the world.

China

Summer in China is from June to August, featuring hot and wet weather. Located in the northern hemisphere, China gets the most direct solar radiation on June 22nd, making China one of the hottest countries in the world.

Yunnan is an ideal destination for a summer trip in China. Owing to its 2,000-meter-altitude with abundant heat and light, Yunnan has a year-round spring-like climate.

Chengdu is a city of pandas. The lovely giant pandas are the No. 1 reason that people visit Chengdu.

Guilin is famous for its marvelous limestone scenery. It's a city sitting amid karst mountains. The picturesque Li River takes you into a misty Chinese brush painting.

Zhangjiajie features fabulous mountainous scenery. It is a natural summer resort and has China's first national forest park.

Indonesia

Indonesia is home to a unique culture and breathtaking landscapes. Summer months here are characterized by warm temperatures and low precipitation when compared to the wet season.

The Borobudur Temple is the ideal place to experience the country's spiritual heritage and history. The temple is decorated with 504 Buddha statues and is home to some of the world's most complex stone carvings.

Visiting the Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary in Ubud, Bali, is one of the many unique things to do in Indonesia in summer. The forest is home to more than a thousand Balinese macaque monkeys as well as three temples.

The Komodo National Park is home to the world's largest lizard, known as the Komodo Dragon. The park is one of the best places to see the Komodo Dragon, attracting tourists who wish to witness this amazing creature.

Exploring the Gili Islands is one way to get away from the crowds and experience a quiet summer vacation in Indonesia.
