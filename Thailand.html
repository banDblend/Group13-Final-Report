Overview

Thailand, country located in the centre of mainland Southeast Asia. Located wholly within the tropics, Thailand encompasses diverse ecosystems, including the hilly forested areas of the northern frontier, the fertile rice fields of the central plains, the broad plateau of the northeast, and the rugged coasts along the narrow southern peninsula. Thailand was primarily an agricultural country, but since the 1960s increasing numbers of people have moved to Bangkok, the capital, and to other cities. Although the greater Bangkok metropolitan area remains the preeminent urban centre in the country, there are other sizable cities, such as Chiang Mai in the north, Nakhon Ratchasima (Khorat), Khon Kaen, and Udon Thani in the northeast, Pattaya in the southeast, and Hat Yai in the far south.

Budget

Thailand is a well-liked travel destination and one of the most reasonably priced. Though it's among the more reasonable nations in Southeast Asia, overall, it's more expensive than Vietnam, Laos, Cambodia, Myanmar, and Indonesia (not including Bali). Budget tourists should factor roughly around $30 each day, or $210, for a week's worth of vacation in Thailand (not including airfare). A hostel with dorm rooms is the least expensive place to stay, costing roughly $8 per night. For around $12 per night, you may also stay in homestays, where you will be housed with a local family. For local transit, consider using one of the less than $1 local buses or the $2 songthaews, which are red passenger vehicles that are frequently used for local travel. For food, explore the native cuisine at street vendors and at night markets. Seek out free monasteries and temples to visit rather than well-known ones that charge admission.
